Game Theory: Put yourself in others' shoes

发表于2025-08-25|更新于2025-08-28
|总字数:573|阅读时长:3分钟|浏览量:

Lock people up in classroom and tell them they should be better people. – Maoist

Prisoners Dilemma 囚徒困境

Example

  • joint project (联合项目) == incentive to shirk (逃避责任) 例如寝室卫生、集体劳动

  • price competition (价格战) == incentive to undercut price (collusion 勾结、共谋、串通)

  • common resource (自然资源) == incentive to “overfish” or pollute the issue of carbon emission

Remedies 解决措施

Not joint communication

  1. contracts
  2. treaties
  3. regulations
  4. repeat play
  5. education - Maoist

Core: Change payoff



Let’s play games!

Please write a number of 1~100 to compete for 5 yuan, and there are several other people who are also competing with you. The rule is: summarize everyone’s numbers, take 2/3 of the average, and finally the person closest to this number will receive the prize.

请写一个1～100的数字来竞争5元，同时还有其他若干人跟你一起同样在竞争。规则是：汇总所有人的数字，取平均值的2/3，最终跟这个数字最接近的人获得奖金。

Formal Stuff

Ingredients of a Game natation # in game
players $i, j$ you all
startegies $s_i$, a particular strategy of player $i$ $eg.$ 13
$S_i$, the set of possible strategies of player $i$ {1, …, 100}
$s$, a particular play of the game
a strategy profile (策略组合)		 the spreed sheet/list/vector
payoffs $u_i(s_1, …, s_i, …, s_N)$ or $u_i(s)$ ￥5  if win
0  otherwise

Assume known

One more notation, $s_{-i}$ is a choice for all except person $i$, just like $u_i(s_i, s_{-i})$.


Game Example

$L$ $C$ $R$
$T$ (5, -1) (11, 3) (0, 0)
$B$ (6, 4) (0, 2) (2, 0)

The Game is not symmetric in the payoffs or in the strategies.

非对称博弈

  • players: 1, 2
  • strategies: $S_1 = [T, B]$
    $S_2 = [L, C, R]$
  • payoffs: eg. $u_1(T, C) = 11$, $u_2(T, C) = 3$

严格劣势策略的符号表示：

Define   Player $i$’s strategy $s_i^{‘}$ is strictly dominated by player $i$’s strategy $s_i$ if $u_i(s_i, s_{-i}) > u_i(s_i^{‘}, s_{-i})$ for all $s_{-i}$.


弱劣势策略的符号表示：

Define   Player $i$’s strategy $s_i^{‘}$ is weakly dominated by player $i$’s strategy $s_i$ if
$u_i(s_i, s_{-i}) \geq u_i(s_i^{‘}, s_{-i})$ for all $s_{-i}$.
$u_i(s_i, s_{-i}) > u_i(s_i^{‘}, s_{-i})$ for some $s_{-i}$.

$>30$   “in shoe, in shoe”

Why we Get 21 ?

If everyone we choose 1, all we get the money. But why?

Because we cannot avoid playing games for the first time, some people may overlook the situation of multiple games. If we were to play this game again now, I believe most people would choose numbers within 10.
